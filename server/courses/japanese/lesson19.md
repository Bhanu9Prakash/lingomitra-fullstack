## Lesson 19: Expressing Opinions & Feelings - Japanese Emotional Language

Being able to express your opinions, feelings, and emotions is essential for meaningful communication. In this lesson, we'll explore various ways to express what you think and how you feel in Japanese.

### Expressing Opinions with と思います (*to omoimasu*)

The most common way to express an opinion in Japanese is to use the pattern:

**[Statement] + と思います。**
*[Statement] + to omoimasu.*
"I think that [statement]."

**Examples:**

* 日本語は難しいと思います。
* *Nihongo wa muzukashii to omoimasu.*
* "I think Japanese is difficult."

* 明日は雨が降ると思います。
* *Ashita wa ame ga furu to omoimasu.*
* "I think it will rain tomorrow."

* 田中さんは来ないと思います。
* *Tanaka-san wa konai to omoimasu.*
* "I don't think Mr. Tanaka will come." (Literally: "I think Mr. Tanaka won't come.")

> **Thinking Point:** The と (*to*) particle works like quotation marks, indicating that what comes before it is what you think. 思います (*omoimasu*) means "to think."

For the past tense (thought), use と思いました (*to omoimashita*):

* 映画は面白いと思いました。
* *Eiga wa omoshiroi to omoimashita.*
* "I thought the movie was interesting."

### Expressing Stronger Opinions and Beliefs

**～と信じています (*~to shinjite imasu*) - "I believe that..."**
* 彼は無実だと信じています。
* *Kare wa mujitsu da to shinjite imasu.*
* "I believe he is innocent."

**～と確信しています (*~to kakushin shite imasu*) - "I am convinced that..."**
* この方法が一番良いと確信しています。
* *Kono hōhō ga ichiban yoi to kakushin shite imasu.*
* "I am convinced that this method is the best."

### Expressing Agreement and Disagreement

**Agreement:**
* そうですね。
* *Sō desu ne.*
* "That's right/I agree."

* 全く同感です。
* *Mattaku dōkan desu.*
* "I completely agree."

* おっしゃる通りです。
* *Ossharu tōri desu.*
* "It is as you say." (Polite)

**Disagreement:**
* いいえ、そうではありません。
* *Iie, sō dewa arimasen.*
* "No, that's not right."

* すみませんが、私はそう思いません。
* *Sumimasen ga, watashi wa sō omoimasen.*
* "I'm sorry, but I don't think so."

* 残念ですが、賛成できません。
* *Zannen desu ga, sansei dekimasen.*
* "Unfortunately, I cannot agree."

### Expressing Feelings with Adjectives

Japanese uses a variety of adjectives to express feelings:

**Basic Feelings:**
* 嬉しい (*ureshii*) - happy, glad
* 悲しい (*kanashii*) - sad
* 楽しい (*tanoshii*) - fun, enjoyable
* 寂しい (*sabishii*) - lonely
* 怖い (*kowai*) - scared, frightened
* 恥ずかしい (*hazukashii*) - embarrassed
* 退屈 (*taikutsu*) - bored
* 疲れた (*tsukareta*) - tired
* 驚いた (*odoroita*) - surprised

**Examples:**
* 日本に行けて嬉しいです。
* *Nihon ni ikete ureshii desu.*
* "I'm happy that I can go to Japan."

* 友達が引っ越して寂しいです。
* *Tomodachi ga hikkoshite sabishii desu.*
* "I'm lonely because my friend moved away."

### Expressing Likes and Dislikes

**1. Using 好き (*suki*) and 嫌い (*kirai*):**

* 私は猫が好きです。
* *Watashi wa neko ga suki desu.*
* "I like cats."

* 私はナスが嫌いです。
* *Watashi wa nasu ga kirai desu.*
* "I dislike eggplants."

**2. Expressing Degrees of Liking:**

* 大好き (*daisuki*) - love, really like
* 好き (*suki*) - like
* まあまあ好き (*māmā suki*) - sort of like
* あまり好きじゃない (*amari suki ja nai*) - don't like much
* 嫌い (*kirai*) - dislike
* 大嫌い (*daikirai*) - hate, really dislike

**Examples:**
* 私は日本語が大好きです。
* *Watashi wa nihongo ga daisuki desu.*
* "I love Japanese."

* 彼はホラー映画があまり好きじゃありません。
* *Kare wa horā eiga ga amari suki ja arimasen.*
* "He doesn't like horror movies much."

### Expressing Interest with 興味があります (*kyōmi ga arimasu*)

* 日本文化に興味があります。
* *Nihon bunka ni kyōmi ga arimasu.*
* "I'm interested in Japanese culture."

* 政治にあまり興味がありません。
* *Seiji ni amari kyōmi ga arimasen.*
* "I'm not very interested in politics."

### Expressing Desires with ～たいです (*~tai desu*)

* 日本に行きたいです。
* *Nihon ni ikitai desu.*
* "I want to go to Japan."

* 何が食べたいですか。
* *Nani ga tabetai desu ka?*
* "What do you want to eat?"

### Expressing Hopes with ～といいな/～ばいいな (*~to ii na*/*~ba ii na*)

* 明日は晴れるといいな。
* *Ashita wa hareru to ii na.*
* "I hope it will be sunny tomorrow."

* 早く元気になればいいな。
* *Hayaku genki ni nareba ii na.*
* "I hope you get well soon."

### Expressing Worry and Concern

* 試験に合格できるか心配です。
* *Shiken ni gōkaku dekiru ka shinpai desu.*
* "I'm worried about whether I can pass the exam."

* 彼女の健康が心配です。
* *Kanojo no kenkō ga shinpai desu.*
* "I'm concerned about her health."

### Asking for Opinions

* どう思いますか。
* *Dō omoimasu ka?*
* "What do you think?"

* ～についてどう思いますか。
* *~ni tsuite dō omoimasu ka?*
* "What do you think about ~?"

* ご意見を聞かせてください。
* *Go-iken o kikasete kudasai.*
* "Please tell me your opinion." (Formal)

### Common Mistakes

* **Direct Translation of "Feel"**: Translating "I feel..." directly as 私は感じます (*watashi wa kanjimasu*), which is not natural for expressing emotions in Japanese.
* **Word Order**: Putting the particle が in the wrong position with emotion expressions.
* **Subject Omission**: Forgetting that the subject is often omitted in Japanese conversations when it's clear from context.
* **Incorrect Formality Level**: Using casual expressions in formal situations or vice versa.
