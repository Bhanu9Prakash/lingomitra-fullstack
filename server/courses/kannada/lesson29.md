## Lesson 29: Idiomatic Expressions & Proverbs - Speaking Like a Native

To truly sound natural in Kannada, you need to understand and use idiomatic expressions, proverbs, and figurative language. These colorful phrases often don't translate literally but carry deep cultural significance. In this lesson, we'll explore common Kannada idioms, proverbs, and colloquial expressions that will help you speak more like a native.

### Common Kannada Idioms (ನುಡಿಗಟ್ಟುಗಳು - nuDigaTTugaLu)

Idioms are phrases whose meanings aren't predictable from the words themselves. They're used frequently in everyday conversation:

| Kannada | Transliteration | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning | Usage |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|-------|
| ಕೈ ಕಾಲು ಹಿಡಿಯುವುದು | kai kaalu hiDiyuvudu | To hold someone's hands and feet | To beg/plead with someone | ಅವನು ಉದ್ಯೋಗಕ್ಕಾಗಿ ಮ್ಯಾನೇಜರ್ **ಕೈ ಕಾಲು ಹಿಡಿದ**. (avanu udyogakkaagi myaanejar **kai kaalu hiDida**.) - He pleaded with the manager for a job. |
| ಕನ್ನಡ ಕಳಿ | kannaDa kaLi | To steal Kannada | To learn Kannada improperly | ನಿನ್ನ ಕನ್ನಡ **ಕನ್ನಡ ಕಳಿ** ಆಗಿದೆ. (ninna kannaDa **kannaDa kaLi** aagide.) - Your Kannada is broken/improperly learned. |
| ಮೂಗು ತೂರಿಸು | moogu toorisu | To insert one's nose | To interfere in others' affairs | ಎಲ್ಲದಕ್ಕೂ **ಮೂಗು ತೂರಿಸಬೇಡ**. (elladakkoo **moogu toorisabeeDa**.) - Don't interfere in everything. |
| ಕಿವಿ ಮಾತು | kivi maatu | Ear talk | Gossip/whispering | ಅವರ ಬಗ್ಗೆ **ಕಿವಿ ಮಾತು** ಹೇಳಬೇಡ. (avara bagge **kivi maatu** heLabeeDa.) - Don't gossip about them. |
| ಹಲ್ಲು ಕಿರಿ | hallu kiri | To gnash teeth | To be extremely angry | ಅವನು ನನ್ನ ಮೇಲೆ **ಹಲ್ಲು ಕಿರಿದ**. (avanu nanna meele **hallu kirida**.) - He was furious with me. |
| ಕಣ್ಣು ಕೆಂಪಾಗು | kaNNu kempaagu | Eyes becoming red | To become angry | ನನ್ನ ಮಾತಿಗೆ ಅವನ **ಕಣ್ಣು ಕೆಂಪಾಯಿತು**. (nanna maatige avana **kaNNu kempaayitu**.) - He became angry at my words. |
| ಕೈ ಚಾಚು | kai chaachu | To extend hand | To beg/ask for help | ಬೇರೆಯವರ ಮುಂದೆ **ಕೈ ಚಾಚಬೇಡ**. (bereyavara munde **kai chaachabeeDa**.) - Don't beg in front of others. |
| ಮನಸ್ಸು ಬಿಚ್ಚು | manassu bichchu | To open mind | To speak openly/frankly | ಅವಳು ನನ್ನ ಜೊತೆ **ಮನಸ್ಸು ಬಿಚ್ಚಿ** ಮಾತನಾಡಿದಳು. (avaLu nanna jote **manassu bichchi** maatanaaDiSvaLu.) - She spoke frankly with me. |
| ಹೊಟ್ಟೆಕಿಚ್ಚು | hoTTekichchu | Stomach fire | Jealousy | ಅವನಿಗೆ ನನ್ನ ಯಶಸ್ಸಿನ ಬಗ್ಗೆ **ಹೊಟ್ಟೆಕಿಚ್ಚು**. (avanige nanna yashassina bagge **hoTTekichchu**.) - He is jealous of my success. |
| ನಾಲಿಗೆ ಎಳೆಯು | naalige eLeyu | To pull tongue | To tease someone | ಅವಳು ತನ್ನ ತಂಗಿಯನ್ನು **ನಾಲಿಗೆ ಎಳೆಯುತ್ತಿದ್ದಾಳೆ**. (avaLu tanna tangiyannu **naalige eLeyuttiddaaLe**.) - She is teasing her younger sister. |

### Popular Kannada Proverbs (ಗಾದೆಗಳು - gaadegaLu)

Proverbs contain cultural wisdom and are frequently used to emphasize points in conversation:

| Kannada | Transliteration | Literal Translation | Meaning | Usage |
|---------|-----------------|---------------------|---------|-------|
| ಕಲಿತಷ್ಟೂ ಕಲಿಯಬೇಕು | kalitashToo kaliyabeeku | Learn as much as you learn | Learning is a continuous process | ಯಾವಾಗಲೂ **ಕಲಿತಷ್ಟೂ ಕಲಿಯಬೇಕು** ಎಂಬುದು ನೆನಪಿರಲಿ. (yaavaagaloo **kalitashToo kaliyabeeku** embudu nenapiDi.) - Always remember that learning is a continuous process. |
| ಆನೆ ಬಂತು ಆನೆ ಬಂತು ಅಂದರೆ ನಂಬುವುದಿಲ್ಲ, ಬಂದ ಮೇಲೆ ಓಡುವುದಿಲ್ಲ | aane bantu aane bantu andare nambuvudilla, banda meele oDuvudilla | When told an elephant is coming, don't believe; when it has come, don't run | Don't ignore warnings, but don't panic either | ಮೊದಲೇ ಎಚ್ಚರಿಕೆ ನೀಡಿದ್ದೆ. **ಆನೆ ಬಂತು ಆನೆ ಬಂತು ಅಂದರೆ ನಂಬುವುದಿಲ್ಲ, ಬಂದ ಮೇಲೆ ಓಡುವುದಿಲ್ಲ**. (modalee echcharike neeDide. **aane bantu aane bantu andare nambuvudilla, banda meele oDuvudilla**.) - I warned you earlier. When warned about danger, people don't believe; when it arrives, they can't escape. |
| ಹುಲಿ ಬಾಯಿಗೆ ಬಿದ್ದ ಮೇಲೆ ಹಸುವನ್ನು ಕಾಪಾಡಲಾಗದು | huli baayige bidda meele hasuvannu kaapaaDalaagadu | Can't save the cow after it falls into tiger's mouth | Too late to act after disaster | ನೀವು ತುಂಬಾ ತಡವಾಗಿ ಬಂದಿದ್ದೀರಿ. **ಹುಲಿ ಬಾಯಿಗೆ ಬಿದ್ದ ಮೇಲೆ ಹಸುವನ್ನು ಕಾಪಾಡಲಾಗದು**. (neevu tumbaa taDavaagi bandiddeeri. **huli baayige bidda meele hasuvannu kaapaaDalaagadu**.) - You've come too late. Can't save the situation after disaster has struck. |
| ಕೊಟ್ಟವರು ದೇವರು, ಕೊಂಡವರು ಗಂಡ | koTTavaru devaru, koNDavaru gaNDa | The giver is God, the taker is husband | What is given is divine, what is taken is earned | ನಮಗೆ ಯಾರೂ ಏನನ್ನೂ ಉಚಿತವಾಗಿ ಕೊಡುವುದಿಲ್ಲ. **ಕೊಟ್ಟವರು ದೇವರು, ಕೊಂಡವರು ಗಂಡ**. (namage yaaroo eenannoo uchitavaagi koDuvudilla. **koTTavaru devaru, koNDavaru gaNDa**.) - No one gives us anything for free. What's given is a gift, what's taken is earned. |
| ಕೂತು ಉಂಡವನಿಗೆ ಕುತ್ತಿಗೆ ವರೆಗೆ | kootu uNDavanige kuttige varege | For one who eats sitting, up to the neck | Easy living leads to problems | ಕೆಲಸ ಮಾಡದೆ ಸುಮ್ಮನೆ ಇದ್ದರೆ ಅದರ ಪರಿಣಾಮ ಕಟ್ಟಬೇಕಾಗುತ್ತದೆ. **ಕೂತು ಉಂಡವನಿಗೆ ಕುತ್ತಿಗೆ ವರೆಗೆ**. (kelasa maaDade summane iddare adara pariNaama kaTTabeekaaguttade. **kootu uNDavanige kuttige varege**.) - If you're idle without working, you'll face the consequences. Easy living leads to troubles. |
| ಮಾತಿಗಿಂತ ಕೃತಿ ಮೇಲು | maatiginla kruti meelu | Action is better than words | Actions speak louder than words | ಸುಮ್ಮನೆ ಮಾತನಾಡುವುದಕ್ಕಿಂತ ಏನಾದರೂ ಮಾಡಿ ತೋರಿಸಿ. **ಮಾತಿಗಿಂತ ಕೃತಿ ಮೇಲು**. (summane maatanaaDuvudakinta eenadaroo maaDi toorisi. **maatiginla kruti meelu**.) - Rather than just talking, show through action. Actions speak louder than words. |
| ಬಂದೋಬಸ್ತಿಗೆ ಮಿಂಚಿನ ಕಡಿ | bandobastige minchina kaDi | Lightning strike to preparation | Even the best plans can fail | ನಾವು ಎಷ್ಟೇ ಯೋಜನೆ ಮಾಡಿದರೂ ಕೆಲವೊಮ್ಮೆ ವಿಫಲವಾಗುತ್ತದೆ. **ಬಂದೋಬಸ್ತಿಗೆ ಮಿಂಚಿನ ಕಡಿ**. (naavu eshTee yojane maaDidaroo kelavomme viphalavaaguttade. **bandobastige minchina kaDi**.) - No matter how well we plan, sometimes things fail. Even the best preparations can be disrupted. |

### Everyday Expressions & Colloquialisms

These common phrases are used in daily conversation and will make your Kannada sound more natural:

| Kannada | Transliteration | Meaning | Usage |
|---------|-----------------|---------|-------|
| ಸುಮ್ನಿರು | sumniru | Be quiet/silent | ಸ್ವಲ್ಪ **ಸುಮ್ನಿರು**, ನನಗೆ ಹೆಡ್ಡೇಕ್ ಆಗುತ್ತಿದೆ. (svalpa **sumniru**, nanage heDDeek aaguttide.) - Be quiet for a while, I'm getting a headache. |
| ಹೋಗಿಬಿಡು | hoogibiDu | Just go/leave | ಇಲ್ಲಿ ಯಾಕೆ ನಿಂತಿದ್ದೀಯ? **ಹೋಗಿಬಿಡು**. (illi yaake ninthiddeeya? **hoogibiDu**.) - Why are you standing here? Just go. |
| ಬಿಡಪ್ಪ/ಬಿಡಮ್ಮ | biDappa/biDamma | Leave it/forget it | **ಬಿಡಪ್ಪ**, ಅದರ ಬಗ್ಗೆ ಯೋಚನೆ ಮಾಡಬೇಡ. (**biDappa**, adara bagge yochane maaDabeeDa.) - Forget it, don't think about it. |
| ಮತ್ತೆ ಏನು? | matte eenu? | What else? / So what? | ನಾನು ಬರೋದಿಲ್ಲ. **ಮತ್ತೆ ಏನು**? (naanu baroodilla. **matte eenu**?) - I'm not coming. So what? |
| ಅದೇನೋ ಹೌದು | adeeno haudu | That's true/indeed | **ಅದೇನೋ ಹೌದು**, ನೀವು ಹೇಳಿದ್ದು ಸರಿ. (**adeeno haudu**, neevu heLiddu sari.) - Indeed, what you said is right. |
| ಏನು ಮಾಡಕ್ಕಾಗುತ್ತೆ? | eenu maaDakkaagutte? | What can be done? | ಮಳೆ ಬಂದಿದೆ, **ಏನು ಮಾಡಕ್ಕಾಗುತ್ತೆ**? (maLe bandide, **eenu maaDakkaagutte**?) - It's raining, what can be done? |
| ನೋಡೋಣ | nooDooNa | Let's see | ನಾಳೆ ಬರ್ತೀನಿ **ನೋಡೋಣ**. (naaLe barteeni **nooDooNa**.) - I'll come tomorrow, let's see. |
| ಗೊತ್ತಿಲ್ವಾ? | gottilvaa? | Don't you know? | ಅವನು ನನ್ನ ಅಣ್ಣ **ಗೊತ್ತಿಲ್ವಾ**? (avanu nanna aNNa **gottilvaa**?) - He's my brother, don't you know? |
| ಸುಮ್ನೆ ಬಿಡು | sumne biDu | Just leave it | ಅದರ ಬಗ್ಗೆ **ಸುಮ್ನೆ ಬಿಡು**. (adara bagge **sumne biDu**.) - Just leave it about that matter. |
| ಏನಿದೆ? | eenide? | What's up? | **ಏನಿದೆ**? ಯಾಕೆ ಚಿಂತೆಯಲ್ಲಿ ಕಾಣ್ತಿದೀಯಾ? (**Eenide**? yaake chinteyalli kaaNtideeyaa?) - What's up? Why do you look worried? |

### Emotional & Emphatic Expressions

These expressions convey emotions and emphasis in Kannada:

| Kannada | Transliteration | Meaning | Usage |
|---------|-----------------|---------|-------|
| ಅಯ್ಯೋ! | ayyoo! | Oh no!/Alas! | **ಅಯ್ಯೋ**! ನನ್ನ ಫೋನ್ ಬಿದ್ದು ಹೋಯಿತು. (**Ayyoo**! nanna phon biddu hoyitu.) - Oh no! My phone fell. |
| ಅಬ್ಬಾ! | abbaa! | Wow!/Phew! | **ಅಬ್ಬಾ**! ತುಂಬಾ ಒಳ್ಳೆಯ ಊಟ! (**Abbaa**! tumbaa oLLeya ooTa!) - Wow! What a great meal! |
| ಛೇ! | chhee! | Tch!/Ugh! | **ಛೇ**! ನನ್ನಿಂದ ತಪ್ಪಾಯಿತು. (**Chhee**! nanninda tappaayitu.) - Ugh! I made a mistake. |
| ಎಷ್ಟು ಚೆನ್ನಾಗಿದೆ! | eshTu chennaagide! | How nice/beautiful! | **ಎಷ್ಟು ಚೆನ್ನಾಗಿದೆ** ಈ ಜಾಗ! (**EshTu chennaagide** ee jaaga!) - How beautiful this place is! |
| ಏನು ಮಾತು! | eenu maatu! | What a thing to say! | **ಏನು ಮಾತು**! ನೀನು ಹೇಗೆ ಹಾಗೆ ಹೇಳಬಹುದು? (**Eenu maatu**! neenu hege haage heLabahudu?) - What a thing to say! How could you say that? |
| ದೇವರೇ! | devare! | Oh God! | **ದೇವರೇ**! ನನಗೆ ಸಹಾಯ ಮಾಡು. (**Devare**! nanage sahaaya maaDu.) - Oh God! Help me. |
| ಹೋಗಲಿ ಬಿಡು | hoogali biDu | Let it go | **ಹೋಗಲಿ ಬಿಡು**, ಬೇಡ, ಮರೆತುಬಿಡಿ. (**Hoogali biDu**, beeDa, maretubiDi.) - Let it go, forget about it. |
| ಸುಮ್ಮನೆ! | summane! | Just like that! | ನೀನು **ಸುಮ್ಮನೆ** ಕೇಳ್ತಾ ಇದೀಯಾ? (neenu **summane** keLtaa ideeyaa?) - Are you just asking like that? |

### Figurative Language and Metaphors

Kannada uses rich figurative language in everyday speech:

| Kannada | Transliteration | Literal Meaning | Figurative Meaning | Example |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|-------------------|---------|
| ಕಲ್ಲಿನ ಹೃದಯ | kallina hrudaya | Stone heart | Heartless or emotionless person | ಅವಳಿಗೆ **ಕಲ್ಲಿನ ಹೃದಯ**. ಯಾರ ನೋವನ್ನೂ ನೋಡುವುದಿಲ್ಲ. (avaLige **kallina hrudaya**. yaara novannu nooDuvudilla.) - She has a heart of stone. She doesn't see anyone's pain. |
| ಬಾಯಿಗೆ ಬಂದಂತೆ | baayige bandante | As it comes to mouth | Speaking without thinking | ಅವನು **ಬಾಯಿಗೆ ಬಂದಂತೆ** ಮಾತನಾಡುತ್ತಾನೆ. (avanu **baayige bandante** maatanaaDuttaane.) - He speaks whatever comes to his mouth. |
| ಕಣ್ಣಿಗೆ ಕಣ್ಣಿಲ್ಲ | kaNNige kaNNilla | No eye for eye | Not caring about consequences | ಅವನಿಗೆ **ಕಣ್ಣಿಗೆ ಕಣ್ಣಿಲ್ಲ**, ಏನಾದರೂ ಮಾಡಬಲ್ಲ. (avanige **kaNNige kaNNilla**, eenadaroo maaDaballa.) - He doesn't care about consequences, he can do anything. |
| ಕುರಿ ಮೇಲೆ ತೋಳ | kuri mele toLa | Wolf over sheep | Predatory behavior | ಈ ಕಂಪನಿ **ಕುರಿ ಮೇಲೆ ತೋಳ**. ಎಲ್ಲರನ್ನೂ ಶೋಷಿಸುತ್ತಾರೆ. (ee kampani **kuri mele toLa**. ellarannu shoshisuttaare.) - This company is like a wolf over sheep. They exploit everyone. |
| ಕಿವಿಯಿಂದ ಕಿವಿಗೆ | kiviyinda kivige | From ear to ear | Spreading gossip | ಈ ಮಾತು **ಕಿವಿಯಿಂದ ಕಿವಿಗೆ** ಹೋಗಬಾರದು. (ee maatu **kiviyinda kivige** hoogabaaradu.) - This matter should not spread from ear to ear. |

### How to Use Idioms Appropriately

When using idioms and proverbs, consider:

1. **Context**: Use idioms appropriate to the situation. Some may be too informal for professional settings.

2. **Age and Relationship**: Some expressions may be disrespectful when used with elders.

3. **Region**: Be aware that some expressions vary by region within Karnataka.

4. **Timing**: Proverbs work best when they naturally fit the conversation, not when forced.

5. **Cultural Awareness**: Many idioms reflect cultural values and beliefs.

### Common Mistakes with Idioms

* **Literal Translation**: Avoid translating English idioms directly into Kannada - use authentic Kannada expressions instead.

* **Overuse**: Using too many idioms in one conversation can sound unnatural.

* **Mixing Registers**: Some idioms are very colloquial and may not fit in formal contexts.

* **Misinterpreting Meaning**: Make sure you understand the true meaning of an idiom before using it.
